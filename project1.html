About the Project

Overstay is a first-person puzzle game developed during a 7-week Vertical Slice course at Uppsala University. The game challenges players‚Äô perception of time through a unique time-manipulation mechanic set inside the mysterious Pontem Hotel, a place built for wandering spirits.

As a guest who has overslept their checkout, you find yourself trapped in the hotel after a sudden power overload. To escape, you must use your wits and manipulate time to solve intricate puzzles while repairing the hotel‚Äôs spirit power system.

The central mechanic allows players to record, replay, and delete timelines of their own actions. By interacting with these recordings, players can cooperate with past versions of themselves in real-time to progress, creating clever and layered puzzle-solving opportunities.

Each successful repair unlocks new floors of the hotel and reveals its hidden secrets. With its combination of atmosphere, mystery, and innovative mechanics, Overstay delivers a mind-bending experience where only your creativity and strategic thinking will determine if you can escape the Land-in-between.

My Contribution

For Overstay, I focused on Level Design and acted as the team's main Quality Assurance (QA) lead.

Level Design

  • Designed multiple levels and contributed to the overall puzzle flow of the game, ensuring that each challenge tied into the time-manipulation mechanic.
  • Created blockouts, iterated on layouts through extensive playtesting, and polished the final rooms with storytelling elements such as cables that lit up after puzzle completion and environmental details that reinforced player progress.
  • Balanced gameplay by integrating puzzles that required cooperation with past timelines, while decorating hallways and rooms to provide a cohesive player experience.

Quality Assurance

  • Served as the primary QA tester, conducting bug-testing across gameplay, visuals, controls, and onboarding to ensure a smooth player experience.
  • Built and maintained a structured QA pipeline: teammates reported issues through a Google survey, which I categorized and logged into a detailed bug database in Excel. This system helped the team track over 180 bugs, with a fix rate of about 95%.
  • Tested puzzle balance to prevent exploits, verified visual consistency, and flagged critical issues that could break progression.

Team Collaboration

  • Collaborated closely with programmers, artists, and fellow level designers, ensuring bugs and design issues were communicated clearly and resolved efficiently.
  • My QA work influenced several design decisions, as I identified problems with puzzle pacing, mechanics, and level layout that led to important adjustments during development.
