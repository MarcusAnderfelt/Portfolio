About the Project

Imagination is a couch co-op platformer for two to four players set in a children’s imagination. Navigate lava-filled fantasies, use items, and cooperate to traverse the levels. Grab your friends to go on an adventurous quest to find the scattered cookies. Just remember — don’t touch the floor!

The game was developed as a case study for two degree projects. One team researched the connection between cooperation and level layout, and the other the impact of visual design on player interest.

Story

You and your friends are having a sleepover, and despite the late hour, you’ve stayed wide awake with one mission in mind: cookies! Knowing how much kids love sweets, the parents have hidden all the cookies. Now, it’s up to you and your friends to team up and find them. But here’s the twist — your imagination transforms the rooms into thrilling worlds full of lava! The quest just got a whole lot more exciting!

Keep the adrenaline pumping by collecting cookies regularly. Each one adds a precious 30 seconds to the level’s timer, ensuring the hunt continues. Use unique items to help your friends avoid the lava and progress swiftly through the rooms. Will you and your team beat the clock and reach your ultimate goal? Chocolate chip cookies are awaiting you!

But remember, when the timer goes out — the lava will rise!

My Contribution

For Imagmination, I worked as both a Game Designer and Programmer, focusing on the creation of tools, obstacles, and underlying systems that tied them together

Design of Tools & Environmental Obstacles

  • Designed and implemented player tools to support cooperative play, including the Slime-Hand (used to pull umbrellas into the level as makeshift platforms or activate levers).
  • Created environmental obstacles such as swinging hammers and rotating pillars, styled to match the childlike aesthetic (e.g., a pencil inside a sharpener as a spinning hazard).
  • Designed unique activators that encouraged teamwork, including:
    • A lever for interactions with a Slime-hand tool
    • A hamster wheel players had to run inside to trigger progression and unlock paths
    • A dartboard that could only be activated by knocking another player into it using the pillow tool
  • Balanced all tools and obstacles for multiplayer to ensure fairness, fun, and cooperation.

Programming in Unreal Engine 5 (Blueprints)

  • Built a modular Activator System in Blueprints, enabling level designers to easily place activators and connect them to obstacles or paths without needing additional code.
  • Programmed and designed the obstacles, activators and tools I created, including the hamster wheel, dartboard, slime-hand, umbrella platform, spinning pencil pillar, domino bridge, and rising platforms.
  • Implemented the death and respawn system: players and tools disappear if they fall into lava, and respawn collectively at the last activated checkpoint.
  • Created the checkpoint system (bells that save progress and respawn location for the whole team).
  • Delivered both prototypes and polished implementations of tools and systems.

Team Collaboration

  • As one of two programmers in a team of seven; I focused on gameplay-specific mechanics and systems, while my teammate handled core systems such as multiplayer controls, cameras, and level select.
  • Worked closely with level designers by creating tools that streamlined their workflow (e.g., the activator system that simplified connecting puzzles to obstacles).
  • Collaborated with artists to ensure visual design and gameplay functionality aligned.
  • Acted as both designer and programmer, with my technical work often shaping design decisions, while always incorporating team feedback to refine ideas.
