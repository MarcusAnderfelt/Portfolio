Concept Art

Concept art project focused on environments, mood, and hub design — exploring how visual storytelling can support level design.

About the Project

This project was part of a Graphics Design course where the goal was to create concept art for a game. For my work, I chose to explore environments and settings with a focus on how concept art can support level design. The idea I developed centered on a child who has run away from home and explores a mysterious forest together with their fox companion. The forest serves as the core environment, while a treehouse hub acts as the child’s home base.

I created a series of concept pieces that visualize this world, including environment mood paintings, a treehouse hub design, and supporting elements like a fox companion and a mood chart featuring the main character.

My Work

Since my main goal was to use concept art as a tool for game design, I focused on creating clear, communicative pieces that could inspire and guide a team:

  • The treehouse hub was my most detailed piece, showing how the player’s base might look and function, with areas for crafting, customization, and fast travel. To achieve accurate proportions and depth, I began with a quick blockout in Unreal Engine, then used digital painting to add detail, character, and atmosphere.
  • I also produced environment explorations in black and white to capture mood, scale, and composition.
  • Finally, I included character sketches, such as the fox companion, to support the theme and showcase how players might emotionally connect with the world.

This project highlights my ability as a game designer who can communicate ideas vissualy. By blending design thinking with digital art, I was able to create concept art that not only captures atmosphere but also clarifies how gameplay spaces and hubs might function.
